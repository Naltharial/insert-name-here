Educate

Built on connecting mutually relevant (similar) content together it seeks to add educational value to the user through linking different forms of related information. Whenever a user encounters an article or post, we add to it the context it so often lacks - related topics, similar articles, social comments, tweets, and so on.

The backend runs a greedy spider network, analyzing articles from various sources and ingesting them into our databases by running them through NLP algorithms for assessing structure and word importance. Once ingested, we use a cosine similarity calculations on word vectors to match articles together and store similarity matrices for the documents.

Contribute

We provide our users with tools to do more research and attach their findings. so the next user that comes along doesn't have to repeat all the work and see what other users have attached. If a piece of content is contributed, it goes through the same NLP process mentioned above, with the addition of reputation information of user that attached it.

This way the time and efford invested into researching q topic can be spread beetwen multiple users and make it easier, more efficient. This is crucial as the amount of content people are bombarded with today is great, thus harder to fact check every little bit.

Reputation

Every entity (ordinary users, publications, journalists) in the platform has a public credibility score called "reputation". Reputation is displayed next to every article source (publications or journalists) that was picked up by our backend, or next to any contribution.

There are many ways to gain reputation, here are some simple examples every user should consider:

  • Connect a verified twitter account
  • Contribute content that other users consider relevant

Some major reputation sources for Publications and journalists

  • Users can answer questions about an article where to help us figure out if the article is compliant with the SPJ Code of Ethics
  • In some cases we rely on 3rd party sources to determent the credibility of an unknown source. (eg. Opensources)
