Goal

We want to bring back trust to the media by forcing them to focus on quality and factual information and not compete in this “clickback” race that is happening at the moment. The solution is to empower the user in making informed judgement quickly. For that, we supply them with the information needed to verify a certain topic or realize its untrustworthiness.

Users are able to do further research on the topic, evaluate news sources and share their contributions with each other. We keep track of everyone's reputation so they can be held accountable for their past and present work. Instead of building a separate system the goal is to be present on existing platforms so user habits can remain intact.

Educate

Related content

We want to add educational value to our users through linking different forms of related information. Whenever a user encounters a piece of content (articles, posts, tweets), we add to it the context it so often lacks - related topics, similar articles, social comments, and so on. to identify entities within the text and connect them to Wikipedia, Wolfram|Alpha or other knowledgebases.

Content processing

Our servers run a greedy spider network, analyzing articles from various sources and ingesting them into our databases by running them through NLP algorithms for assessing structure and word importance. Once ingested, we use similarity calculations on word vectors to match articles together and store similarity matrices for the documents.

Contribute

Article validation

Users can contribute by answering a few simple questions on any given article to helps us asses it's validity and make sure it's written within the Code of Ethics. The weight of the answers depend on the reputation of the user. We use these answer to asses the reputation of the source.

User contributions

Users can directly attach additional links that they consider relevant to the subject. It goes through the same NLP process mentioned above, with the additioni of reputation information of user that attached t. We hope to crowdsource the time and efford that's needed in order to research a given topic. This is crucial if users are to make judgements quickly, but also keep them engaged.

Reputation

Sources

The reputation for an individual source is calculated by aggregating the ratings all of the articles it's associated with. This makes the most significant factor of ones credibility consistency over time, instead of it being judged based on individual basis. The source and it's reputation is displayed next to each piece of content, so the user can take it into account while reading.

Users

Everytime a user makes a contribution he/she stakes his reputation on the line. The more reputation the user has the more his/her contributions matter. If we see a long history of valid ratings and relevant articles we can take that into account in future contributions. And of course the other way around, bad contributions will have a negative on future ratings.

Simple but working prototype, nothing is final

There are many ways to gain reputation, here are some simple examples every user should consider:

  • Connect a verified twitter account
  • Contribute content that other users consider relevant

Some major reputation sources for Publications and journalists

  • Users can answer questions about an article where to help us figure out if the article is compliant with the SPJ Code of Ethics
  • In some cases we rely on 3rd party sources to determent the credibility of an unknown source. (eg. Opensources)

Cross-platform

We don't want to break existing habbits that people have on social media, but rather enrich existing ones. Instead of building a new platform, we will add content directly where news is already consumed. This means no matter the platform, we can always be present, anywhere, everywhere.
